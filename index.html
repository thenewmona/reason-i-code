My coding Journey

With Brooklynn

The reason I started coding

I started coding a little over a year ago, after I lost my job.
I needed to learn a new skill to get a job, and I was blessed to get a scholarship through Udacity.
I am continuing my studies now on my own, to get more profiencent with coding.
I also get the privilege of taking my granddaughter Brooklynn along this journey with me.

Sharing my love of Code with my granddaughter

A big part of my coding journey is going to meetups and workshops.
Here in Grand Rapids, over the past year, there has been a big tech boom, especically in the field of development.
There is also a big push to get children involved, because of this I have been able to take my granddaughter to several of these events.
We recently enjoyed the day at Beer City Code, here in Grand Rapids.
Brooklynn got to play with robots, and hang out with Beau Carnes.
Our summer vacation this year is a whole week at THAT conference, with a stop at the Museum of Science
in Chicago on our way there.

Enpowerment through Education and Encourgement

The biggest thing I have come to appreciate this past year is the awesome community that is out there.
Especially for women devolopers, and newbies. Between Free Code Camp, Women in Development, PyLadies, and other organizations.
There is no shortage of support, encouragement, and accountability.
I get to share all of this with Brooklynn, so as she grows in her coding skills, she will know that she is not alone.
